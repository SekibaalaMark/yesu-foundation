Success Stories

Real stories of transformation and hope from our beneficiaries

Sarah, 18 years old Joined in 2016

From Orphan to University Student

Sarah lost both her parents at the age of 10 and was left to care for her two younger siblings. With no source of income and limited support from relatives, her future looked bleak.

When YESU FOUNDATION identified Sarah and her siblings in 2016, they were enrolled in our sponsorship program. Sarah received educational support, healthcare, and mentorship.

"YESU FOUNDATION didn't just provide for my basic needs; they gave me hope and showed me that I could dream big despite my circumstances. The mentorship I received helped me believe in myself."

Today, Sarah is a first-year university student pursuing a degree in Education. She hopes to become a teacher and give back to her community by educating the next generation.

Her siblings are also thriving in school, with her brother showing exceptional talent in mathematics and her sister excelling in arts.

James, 22 years old Joined in 2015

Building a Future Through Agriculture

James grew up in extreme poverty with his grandmother after his parents separated. Despite his intelligence, he struggled to stay in school due to lack of school fees and often had to work odd jobs to help support his family.

After joining YESU FOUNDATION in 2015, James was able to complete his secondary education. He showed a keen interest in agriculture, and our team encouraged him to pursue this passion.

"The agricultural training I received through YESU FOUNDATION changed my life. I learned modern farming techniques that have helped me create a sustainable source of income for myself and my grandmother."

Through our Better Farming program, James received training in sustainable agriculture and was provided with seeds and tools to start his own small farm. Today, he grows vegetables and raises poultry, which he sells at the local market.

James has become a role model in his community and now mentors other young people interested in agriculture. He plans to expand his farm and create employment opportunities for other youth in his village.

Mary, 15 years old Joined in 2018

Overcoming Health Challenges

Mary was born with a congenital heart defect that required surgery her family couldn't afford. Her condition limited her ability to participate in normal childhood activities and affected her school attendance.

When Mary joined YESU FOUNDATION in 2018, our health team immediately recognized the severity of her condition. Through our healthcare partners, we were able to arrange for her to receive the necessary surgery.

"Before the surgery, I couldn't run or play with my friends. I was always tired and missed a lot of school. Now I can do everything my friends do, and I'm even on the school netball team!"

Following her successful surgery and recovery, Mary has thrived both physically and academically. She now ranks among the top students in her class and dreams of becoming a doctor to help other children with similar conditions.

Mary's story highlights the importance of our Health Promotion program, which ensures that children receive the medical care they need to live healthy, productive lives.

Lukomera Community School Partnership began in 2021

Transforming Education in Lukomera

The Lukomera Community School was struggling with inadequate facilities, limited teaching resources, and low enrollment rates. Many children in the community were not attending school regularly due to these challenges.

In 2021, YESU FOUNDATION partnered with the school to improve educational outcomes for children in the community. We renovated classrooms, provided teaching materials, and implemented teacher training programs.

"The transformation of our school has been remarkable. With better facilities and resources, our teachers are more motivated, and our students are eager to learn. School attendance has improved significantly." - Headteacher, Lukomera Community School

Today, the school has seen a 40% increase in enrollment and a significant improvement in academic performance. The school now serves as a model for other schools in the region.

This success story demonstrates how our community-based approach to education can create lasting change for entire communities, not just individual children.
